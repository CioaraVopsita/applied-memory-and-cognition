Title of the research project: Perception, memory and decision-making

What is the purpose of the study?

The purpose of this study is to find out more about human perception, memory, and decision-making. Only people over the age of 18 have been invited to take part in this study.

Do I have to take part?

Participation is voluntary. You are free to withdraw at any time by closing your internet browser, without giving a reason and without consequence. Should you wish to withdraw your data after completion you can email the researcher, within 72 hours of study completion, quoting your participant ID (no reason needs be provided).

What will happen to me if I take part?

The study requires you to study lists of words, pictures or short video clips on a computer screen. You will then be asked to make decisions about those pictures, words or videos, or remember what those pictures, words or details about the video were. Participation will take no longer than *7 minutes*.

Are there any risks from taking part?

There are no risks, the material to be presented is mundane and not distressing.

Will my taking part in the study be kept confidential?

Your data will not be associated with your name, only with a participant code (prolific ID). No personally identifying information will be collected. Data will be collated on the password protected computers / encrypted hard drive devices of the researchers. Your data will be stored on secure servers at The University of Birmingham.

What will happen to the results of the research study?

The overall findings may be submitted for publication in a scientific journal, or presented at scientific conferences. Following scientific publication, data will be aggregated (combined on spreadsheet) and made available to other researchers in aggregate form. Prolific IDs will not be shared. The data will be preserved and accessible for at least ten years. You will be able to obtain general information about the results of this research by contacting the researcher.

If you have questions or concerns, you may contact the lead researcher, Dr Melissa Colloff, at m.colloff@bham.ac.uk.
